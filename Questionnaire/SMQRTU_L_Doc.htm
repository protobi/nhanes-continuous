National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey

August 2021-August 2023 Data Documentation, Codebook, and Frequencies

Smoking - Recent Tobacco Use (SMQRTU_L)

Data File: SMQRTU_L.xpt

First Published: September 2024
Last Revised: NA

Component Description

 The smoking - recent tobacco use questionnaire (variable name prefix SMQRTU) provides details on use of cigarettes, pipes, cigars, and other forms of tobacco, including e-cigarette use, as well as nicotine replacement therapies in the past 5 days.

Eligible Sample

Participants aged 12 years and older who were able to understand and read English or Spanish were eligible. The interview was not administered to participants needing a proxy informant.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

Questions were self-administered at the Mobile Examination Center (MEC) during the personal (MEC) Interview using the Audio Computer-Assisted Self-Interview (ACASI) system, in English and Spanish only.

Quality Assurance & Quality Control

ACASI systems are programmed with built-in consistency checks to reduce data entry errors.

Data Processing and Editing

Edits were made to ensure completeness, consistency, and analytic usefulness of the data. A variable, SMAQUEX was created as a flag to designate each age group.

Analytic Notes

SMDANY is a summary variable for any tobacco use in the past 5 days. For adults 18+ years, this was based on responses to three questions on tobacco use (smoke, smokeless, nicotine replacement therapy products) (SMQ681, SMQ851, SMQ863). For youths 12-17 years, SMDANY was based on responses to SMQ681 and SMQ851 (smoke and smokeless).

SMQ720: Reports of 40 or more cigarettes per day were assigned a top code of 40.

SMQ690K: Smokeless products were grouped due to limited numbers of counts for specific products. 

Related questions on cigarette and tobacco use for August 2021-August 2023 are located in the SMQ data files. Biomarkers for tobacco exposure, including serum cotinine, are found in NHANES laboratory data files.

Please refer to the NHANES Analytic Guidelines and the on-line NHANES Tutorial for further details on the use of sample weights and other analytic issues.
